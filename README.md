## Overview
Water Works is a mobile and web application designed to help Seattle recreational water users stay informed about local wastewater conditions.

King County operates a combined sewer system, which means sewage and stormwater flow together. During heavy rainfall, the system can become overwhelmed, resulting in Combined Sewer Overflow (CSO) events. These events may release untreated or partially treated wastewater into nearby water bodies, impacting environmental health and public safety.

While tools like the King County CSO status page already exist, they are not always intuitive or accessible for everyday users. Water Works bridges that gap by presenting clear, user-centered information, personalized alerts, and educational resources that empower users to make informed decisions

## Problem Statement

Our Moto is **“How water works for you, how you can work for water”**. Our product is meant to provide a safe and sanitary environment for both our bodies of water and for the local families and people who consistently use recreational water around King County. It started as not many people understand how the wastewater system works / impacts them, so this webapp will help clarify this as well. The main goal of our project is to create a bridge for this gap between recreational users and our water conditions by developing a user-centered approach to communicating conditions within our wastewater system.
