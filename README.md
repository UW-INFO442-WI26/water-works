## Overview
Water Works is a mobile and web application designed to help Seattle recreational water users stay informed about local wastewater conditions.

King County operates a combined sewer system, which means sewage and stormwater flow together. During heavy rainfall, the system can become overwhelmed, resulting in Combined Sewer Overflow (CSO) events. These events may release untreated or partially treated wastewater into nearby water bodies, impacting environmental health and public safety.

While tools like the King County CSO status page already exist, they are not always intuitive or accessible for everyday users. Water Works bridges that gap by presenting clear, user-centered information, personalized alerts, and educational resources that empower users to make informed decisions

## Problem Statement
King County’s wastewater system is a key contributor to our environmental conditions (specifically water conditions) that can affect the health and safety of our local water pipelines and people who use Seattle’s water. There are currently some public-facing tools that exist, like the Combined Sewer Overflow (CSO) status map, which indicates when overflows are occurring, which is correlated to unsafe recreational use. A website like this is meant to serve and inform the community, but isn’t serving people as intended because of different website constraints and a lack of material surrounding the topic. Also, through user research, we found that internal King County staff had trouble with creating a solution that focused on user accessibility. Because of this, recreational users lack insight as to how local water conditions could impact the environment and their own safety. We want to create a bridge for this gap by developing a user-centered approach to communicating conditions within our wastewater system.
